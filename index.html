Georgia is showing a growing European sentiment, particularly evidenced by its pursuit of closer ties with the European Union (EU). One significant indicator is the EU-Georgia Association Agreement, which was signed in 2014 and entered into full force in July 2016.

This agreement aims to deepen political and economic relations between Georgia and the EU, fostering greater integration with European structures and standards.

Furthermore, public opinion surveys have indicated a generally positive attitude towards the EU among Georgian citizens, with many expressing support for closer cooperation and alignment with European values and institutions.
